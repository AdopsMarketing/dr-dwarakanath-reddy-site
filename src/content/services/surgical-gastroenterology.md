Surgical gastroenterology is the operative arm of digestive medicine. It covers planned procedures for benign conditions like gallstones and hernias, as well as high-complexity cases including liver resections, pancreatic surgery, and cancer of the stomach, colon, or rectum.

Every case is evaluated on its own merits. Imaging, endoscopy, and laboratory work are reviewed before any recommendation is made. Where a laparoscopic or minimally invasive approach is feasible, it is offered. Where open surgery is safer, that is recommended instead and the reasoning is explained.
