  - type: steps
    title: "How a laparoscopic distal gastrectomy is performed"
    steps:
      - "Five or six small incisions are made in the upper abdomen under general anaesthesia"
      - "The abdomen is inflated with CO2 to create working space"
      - "The lesser and greater omentum are mobilised; lymph node stations are systematically cleared (D2 dissection for cancer)"
      - "The duodenum is divided distal to the pylorus"
      - "The lower two-thirds of the stomach are divided proximally with a stapler"
      - "Continuity is restored with a stapled or hand-sewn anastomosis (Billroth-II or Roux-en-Y)"
      - "Drains are placed; cuts are closed with absorbable sutures"
    outro: "Total operating time is 3 to 5 hours. The decision between distal, subtotal, and total gastrectomy depends on tumour location and proximal margin requirement."

    title: "Risks and complications"
    body: "Gastrectomy is a major operation. Risks include bleeding, anastomotic leak (the most serious early complication), wound infection, pulmonary complications, dumping syndrome, B12 and iron deficiency long-term, and weight loss. For cancer surgery, oncologic completeness (clear margins and adequate lymph node yield) is the priority. Dr. Reddy discusses procedure-specific risk based on staging and patient fitness."
