    body: "Esophagus surgery is a broad area covering both cancer resection and benign disease. The most common operations are esophagectomy (removal of the cancerous oesophagus with reconstruction), Heller's myotomy (for achalasia), Zenker's diverticulectomy, and large hiatus hernia or paraesophageal hernia repair. The choice of approach — open, thoracoscopic, laparoscopic, or hybrid (Ivor Lewis, McKeown, transhiatal) — depends on tumour location, stage, and patient anatomy."

      - "Zenker's diverticulum — pouch in the upper oesophagus, causing regurgitation and aspiration"

  - type: steps
    title: "How a Heller's myotomy is performed (most common benign procedure)"
    steps:
      - "Five small upper-abdominal incisions are made under general anaesthesia"
      - "The diaphragm is mobilised to expose the lower oesophagus"
      - "The muscle layer of the lower oesophagus is divided lengthwise (myotomy) for 6 to 8 cm above the gastro-oesophageal junction"
      - "The myotomy is extended onto the upper stomach for 2 to 3 cm to relieve the obstruction"
      - "A partial wrap (Dor or Toupet fundoplication) is added to prevent post-operative reflux"
      - "Cuts are closed with absorbable sutures"
    outro: "The procedure takes 90 to 120 minutes. Hospital stay is 2 to 3 nights. Liquid diet for the first week, soft diet for the second, gradually advancing to normal."
